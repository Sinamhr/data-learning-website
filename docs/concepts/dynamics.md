**Dynamics** asks whether the relationship you want to model is stable or changes over time.

If you’re unsure, you can skip this question.

## Options in this tool

### Mostly stable (stationary)
The data distribution and relationships are broadly stable.  
**Examples:** a fixed manufacturing process, stable sensor setup.

### Changes over time (non-stationary)
The data changes (drift, seasonality, policy changes, new sensors, changing user behaviour).  
**Examples:** social media content, economic systems, climate time series, deployment feedback loops.

## Why it matters
Non-stationary settings often need:
- time-aware validation (avoid leakage),
- monitoring after deployment,
- adaptation strategies or continual learning,
- robustness and uncertainty tracking.
